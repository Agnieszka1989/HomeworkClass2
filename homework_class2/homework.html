EIGHT-THOUSANDERS

The eight-thousanders are the 14 independent mountains on Earth that are more than 8,000 metres (26,247 ft) high above sea level.
The mountains are all located in the Himalayan and Karakoram ranges in China, India, Nepal and Pakistan.

List of 14
  • Everest
  • K2
  • Kangchenjunga
  • Lhotse
  • Makalu
  • Cho Oyu
  • Dhaulagiri I
  • Gasherbrum II
  • Broad Peak
  • Gasherbrum I
  • Nanga Parbat
  • Manaslu
  • Annapurna I
  • Shishapangma

First people climbed all 14 eight-thousanders:

  1. 1986 Italian Reinhold Messner
  2. 1987 Polish Jerzy Kukuczka
  3. 1995 Swiss Erhard Loretan
